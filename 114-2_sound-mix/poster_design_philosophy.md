# Signal Atelier

Signal Atelier treats a poster as an acoustic control room translated into paper. Space is divided with the calm authority of an engineered console: major fields hold the large claims, smaller compartments carry evidence, and thin lines behave like signal paths. The design should feel meticulously crafted, as if every block has been aligned after countless adjustments by someone who understands both typography and sound.

Color is not decoration but a routing system. Deep navy carries institutional authority, warm paper tones carry explanation, and electric green, cyan, amber, and red operate as signal indicators. The work should look labored over with master-level attention: no accidental gaps, no weak hierarchy, no casual placement. Each color should suggest a measurable sound action: input, processing, bus, output, reflection.

Photography and diagrams are treated as evidence objects. A studio image is not a background; it is a proof of place. A waveform, speaker map, Bus/Stem diagram, or Dry/Wet chart is not an illustration; it is a trace of an invisible process becoming legible. The final image should be painstakingly composed so that abstract workflows feel physically inspectable.

Typography must be sparse but decisive. Large Chinese title forms should anchor the poster with institutional clarity, while small labels behave like technical markings on equipment. Text is essential-only, integrated into the grid, never allowed to become a document pasted onto a poster. The work must appear to come from deep expertise and careful refinement, with every line break, margin, and label tuned.

The conceptual reference is the act of mixing itself: separate sources becoming an intelligible output. The poster should quietly quote the logic of a mixing session through columns, buses, stages, meters, and routed evidence. Anyone familiar with film sound should feel the system immediately; everyone else should still see a pristine, highly crafted visual artifact.
